## Why This Curriculum Exists

Children make thousands of decisions long before anyone teaches them how. They absorb gut instincts and cultural defaults without understanding why those shortcuts work — or when they fail catastrophically.

Decision literacy changes the equation. The same mental models that drive good judgment in science, medicine, engineering, and policy are accessible to young learners when presented through hands-on activities, concrete examples, and guided reflection.

**The goal:** give kids a genuine toolkit for understanding uncertainty, recognising their own cognitive biases, and systematically improving how they make choices — so that by the time the stakes are high, clear thinking is already a habit.

## About the Curriculum

This curriculum teaches kids *how* to decide, not *what* to decide. It builds **five core mental models** that form the foundation of good decision-making:

1. **Probabilistic Thinking** — the world runs on likelihoods, not certainties
2. **Heuristics & Biases** — our brains take shortcuts that sometimes mislead
3. **Opportunity Cost** — every choice has a hidden price tag
4. **Diminishing Returns** — more isn't always better
5. **Iterative Decision-Making** — design → test → measure → revise → repeat

The backbone of the programme is the **Decision Journal** — a running record where students document their reasoning *before* seeing the outcome. Over 18 weeks it becomes a powerful tool for honest self-reflection and visible growth.

Lessons include **age differentiation** with guidance tailored for younger (ages 8–9) and older (ages 10–12) learners, so the same material works across the full age range.

## Curriculum Structure

The curriculum progresses through five phases, plus optional extension material:

| Phase | Weeks | Focus |
|-------|-------|-------|
| Probability & The Physics of Choice | 1–4 | Randomness, probability, process vs. outcome, launching the Decision Journal |
| Debugging the Hardware | 5–8 | Heuristics, loss aversion, sunk costs, framing, bias hunting |
| Data & Signal Processing | 9–11 | Expected value, signal vs. noise, reversible vs. irreversible decisions |
| Game Theory & Social Systems | 12–14 | Ripple effects, Prisoner's Dilemma, Tragedy of the Commons |
| The Optimisation Project | 15–18 | Identify a real problem, design a protocol, test it, iterate |
| Advanced Topics (Optional) | Extension | Bayesian updating, decision trees & multi-step strategy |

### Weekly Format

Each week includes three sessions:

- **Two guided sessions** — adult-led, ~30 minutes each
- **One independent practice** — student-led activity and Decision Journal entry

The curriculum emphasises exploration, reasoning, hands-on activities, and real-world application throughout.
